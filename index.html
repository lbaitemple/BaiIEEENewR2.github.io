Academic Leadership Grounded in IEEE Service

Dr. Li Bai is a tenured Professor and Associate Dean for Research, Graduate Studies & Faculty Affairs in the College of Engineering at Temple University. He has built a leadership record that spans higher education, technical research, and sustained IEEE service at both section and regional levels.

As Region 2 Student Activities Chair, he helped unify student activity operations across Regions 1 and 2, chaired three joint conferences that reactivated post-pandemic engagement, and created surplus funds to support future programs. He has also been recognized by the IEEE Philadelphia Section with the Young Engineer of the Year, Engineer of the Year, and Section Service Awards.

Statement

Harnessing Differences as a Shared Strength

From New York City to Washington, D.C. to underserved counties across the joint region, Dr. Bai's platform focuses on unity, efficiency, and practical value for members.

One Unified Region

The diversity of Regions 1 and 2 is an asset. Dr. Bai's vision is to unite members across major cities, research centers, and underserved communities around IEEE's mission of serving humanity through engineering.

Shared Resources, Tangible Benefits

As Director, he will pool resources across both regions, reduce redundant efforts, and turn collaboration into stronger mentorship networks, broader industry connections, and more accessible professional growth.

Results-Driven Representation

Dr. Bai's record shows that regional unity works in practice: profitable joint conferences, revived student engagement, and advocacy that ensures engineers have a voice in the policies shaping the profession.

Better Together: Regions 1 & 2

Our joint region is a study in contrasts: the prosperity and dynamism of New York City, the policy influence of Washington, D.C., and communities that remain deeply underserved.

Those differences are not barriers. They create the opportunity to share talent, ideas, and opportunity across sections so that every member benefits, regardless of geography or career stage.

Innovation in Action

Bringing Robotics & AI Across the Region

Dr. Bai has expanded regional reach with hands-on robotics and technical training initiatives that connect IEEE members to emerging tools and global collaborators.

MiniPupper Robotics Workshop

Dr. Bai has led MiniPupper robot workshops across Regions 1 and 2, using hands-on robotics experiences to inspire students and volunteers around AI, ROS, and practical engineering problem solving.

He is also collaborating with IEEE Region 10 on international robotics activities, including a Tug-of-War challenge using robotic dogs, to create cross-regional opportunities that strengthen the global IEEE community.
